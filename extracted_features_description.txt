─── HEART RATE & HRV (Heart Rate Variability) ─────────────────

HR          Heart Rate (beats per minute)
RR          R-peak to R-peak interval (time between consecutive heartbeats)
SDNN        Standard Deviation of NN (Normal-to-Normal) intervals
            → Measures overall HRV
RMSSD       Root Mean Square of Successive Differences
            → Measures short-term HRV (parasympathetic activity)
pNN50       Percentage of successive NN intervals differing by more than 50 ms
            → Reflects vagal (parasympathetic) tone
pNN20       Percentage of successive NN intervals differing by more than 20 ms
            → More sensitive version of pNN50
CV          Coefficient of Variation (std / mean)
            → Normalized variability measure
IQR         Interquartile Range (75th percentile - 25th percentile)
            → Robust measure of spread
BBI         Breath-to-Breath Interval (time between consecutive breaths)
QTc         Corrected QT interval (Bazett's formula: QT / sqrt(RR))


─── ECG MORPHOLOGY ─────────────────────────────────────────────

P wave      Atrial depolarization wave
Q wave      First downward deflection of QRS complex
R wave      Main upward deflection of QRS complex (tallest peak)
S wave      Downward deflection after R wave
T wave      Ventricular repolarization wave

PT interval Time from P-wave onset to T-wave end
             → Covers atrial depolarization through ventricular repolarization
QS interval Time from Q-wave to S-wave (= QRS duration)
             → Ventricular depolarization time
             → Normal: 80-120 ms; Wide QRS suggests conduction abnormality
QT interval Time from Q-wave to T-wave end
             → Total ventricular electrical activity duration
             → Prolonged QT: risk of arrhythmia
QTc Bazett  QT interval corrected for heart rate using Bazett's formula
             → QTc = QT / sqrt(RR_seconds)


─── ECG SIGNAL FEATURES ────────────────────────────────────────

R-amp       R-peak amplitude (height of the R-wave)
P2P         Peak-to-Peak amplitude (max - min of signal)
RMS         Root Mean Square (sqrt of mean of squared values)
             → Measures signal power/magnitude
ZCR         Zero Crossing Rate (how often signal crosses zero per sample)
MAV         Mean Absolute Value (mean of |signal|)
meandist    Mean distance between successive differences (vitalwave metric)


─── RESPIRATION ────────────────────────────────────────────────

Resp Rate   Respiratory Rate (breaths per minute)
AMPD        Automatic Multiscale-based Peak Detection (algorithm name)
MSPTD       Modified Smoothed Peak and Trough Detection (algorithm name)
Feets       Trough/valley points in respiration signal (expiration endpoints)
Tidal Excursion  Amplitude difference between peak and preceding trough
                  → Proxy for tidal volume (depth of breath)
Envelope    Signal envelope extracted via Hilbert transform
             → Captures amplitude modulation of the signal


─── IMU (Inertial Measurement Unit) ────────────────────────────

IMU         Inertial Measurement Unit (accelerometer + gyroscope)
Acc         Accelerometer (measures linear acceleration, m/s²)
Gyr         Gyroscope (measures angular velocity, °/s or rad/s)
X/Y/Z       Three orthogonal axes of measurement
Mag         Magnitude (sqrt(x² + y² + z²))
             → Combined 3-axis magnitude

Ribs IMU    IMU sensor placed on the rib cage
Chest IMU   IMU sensor placed on the chest


─── STATISTICAL FEATURES ──────────────────────────────────────

mean        Arithmetic mean (average)
std         Standard deviation
var         Variance (std²)
min         Minimum value
max         Maximum value
median      Middle value when sorted
range       max - min
iqr         Interquartile Range (Q3 - Q1)
skewness    Measure of distribution asymmetry
             → 0 = symmetric, positive = right tail, negative = left tail
kurtosis    Measure of distribution tail heaviness (excess kurtosis)
             → 0 = normal, positive = heavy tails, negative = light tails
rms         Root Mean Square
energy      Sum of squared values (total signal energy)
zcr         Zero Crossing Rate
mav         Mean Absolute Value
p5/p25/p75/p95  5th/25th/75th/95th percentile values


─── SIGNAL QUALITY INDICES (SQI) ──────────────────────────────

SQI         Signal Quality Index
SNR         Signal-to-Noise Ratio (in dB)
             → Higher = cleaner signal
dB          Decibels (logarithmic scale for power ratios)
PSD         Power Spectral Density
             → Distribution of signal power across frequencies
BW          Baseline Wander (low-frequency drift in ECG)
LOA         Limits of Agreement (Bland-Altman: mean ± 1.96*SD)


─── COMPARISON METRICS ─────────────────────────────────────────

abs_diff    Absolute Difference (|device - reference|)
pct_diff    Percentage Difference (|device - reference| / |reference| × 100)
Bland-Altman  Plot of difference vs mean for agreement analysis
Correlation   Pearson/Spearman correlation between signals
ALMA        Arnaud Legoux Moving Average (smoothed trend line)
R²          Coefficient of Determination (goodness of fit)


─── TEMPERATURE ────────────────────────────────────────────────

°C          Degrees Celsius
Fever       Body temperature > 37.5°C
High Fever  Body temperature > 38.5°C
Hypothermia Body temperature < 35.0°C
Slope       Rate of temperature change over time


─── FREQUENCY BANDS ────────────────────────────────────────────

ECG signal band:        0.5 – 40 Hz
QRS complex band:       5 – 15 Hz
Breathing band:         0.1 – 0.5 Hz
Baseline wander:        < 0.5 Hz
HP / LP filter:         High-Pass / Low-Pass filter
SOS                     Second-Order Sections (filter implementation)
